Urban Planning & Land Use Reform

Building Equitable, Sustainable, and Climate-Resilient Communities

Urban land use in America has long been shaped by exclusionary policies that prioritized segregation, sprawl, and car dependency. This plan reverses that legacy by centering equity, affordability, and climate resilience in our cities' design.

Core Strategies

  • Eliminate single-family-only zoning: Legalize duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes everywhere.
  • Legalize accessory dwelling units (ADUs): Allow backyard cottages and basement apartments by right.
  • Abolish minimum parking requirements: Free up space for housing and reduce car dependency.
  • Reform zoning codes: Replace outdated setback, lot size, and height restrictions.
  • Adopt form-based codes: Prioritize walkability and design over land use separation.
  • Protect residents from displacement: Pair reforms with tenant protections and land trusts.
  • Incentivize transit-oriented development: Support density near transit hubs.
  • Repurpose underused land: Convert vacant lots and parking to community assets.

Policy Toolkit

  • Inclusionary zoning for affordable housing
  • Density bonuses for green building and proximity to transit
  • Public acquisition of abandoned properties
  • Urban growth boundaries to limit sprawl
  • Fast-track green retrofits and adaptive reuse

Why This Matters

Land use reform addresses the root causes of climate change, racial segregation, and housing inequality. Compact, mixed-use development lowers carbon emissions, fosters social integration, and reduces municipal costs. These strategies also enhance public health, support local economies, and build safer, more walkable communities.

Integrated Strategy

This approach aligns with broader climate efforts like public transit expansion, environmental remediation, and equitable job creation—forming a cohesive blueprint for transformation.
