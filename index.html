Let’s Be the Adults in the Room

We demand accountability and justice. Fossil fuel companies—long aware of the harm they cause—must be held responsible for the damage they have unleashed. Planetary Health and Systemic Transformation

What We Mean by Planetary Health

Planetary health means safeguarding the conditions that allow humanity and ecosystems to thrive—reconnecting human health, social systems, and environmental stewardship. It demands bold action to heal the rifts between people and nature in the Anthropocene.

The Green New Deal and Beyond

The Green New Deal provides a foundation—but we go further, calling for a transformation rooted in interconnection, social equity, regenerative economies, and environmental justice, as emphasized by the Planetary Health Alliance and the São Paulo Declaration.

Why This Matters Now

We are already exceeding critical planetary boundaries—from climate change to biodiversity loss. Our work isn’t just about cutting emissions. It’s about restoring balance, healing landscapes, protecting the vulnerable, and reimagining prosperity.

What We Demand

  • Governance that integrates social, ecological, and economic goals
  • Education that fosters planetary literacy and interconnection with nature
  • Justice that centers frontline and marginalized communities
  • Cooperative, post-growth economies that prioritize well-being over profit
  • A new planetary ethic of care, regeneration, and responsibility
