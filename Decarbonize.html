Decarbonization, Industry Transformation, and Green Energy

Building a Just, Post-Carbon Economy

Achieving climate stability requires phasing out fossil fuels and transforming energy, industry, and infrastructure. This transition will be equitable, democratic, and focused on community ownership and public benefit.

Core Strategies

  • Phase out fossil fuels: Enact binding emissions caps, revoke new oil and gas permits, and end fossil fuel subsidies.
  • Build a 100% renewable public energy system: Expand wind, solar, geothermal, and storage with community ownership and grid modernization.
  • Decarbonize heavy industry: Impose standards on steel, cement, and chemicals; advance green hydrogen and circular economy practices.
  • Electrify buildings and transport: Retrofit buildings, replace gas appliances, and deploy electric transit fleets, prioritizing low-income communities.
  • Support fossil fuel regions and workers: Provide investment, retraining, and wage guarantees to ensure a just transition.

Why It Matters

Decarbonization isn’t just about cutting emissions—it’s about reshaping systems for justice, clean air, resilient economies, and a regenerative future that serves people and the planet.
