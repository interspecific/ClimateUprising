Reimagining Society

Degrowth, Humanism, and Environmental Stewardship

To build a just and sustainable future, we must move beyond extractive growth toward an economy centered on well-being, care, and ecological balance. This vision redefines abundance and centers stewardship over exploitation.

Core Principles

  • Redefine progress: Replace GDP with measures of well-being, equity, and resilience.
  • Prioritize care and time: Shorten work weeks, expand universal basic services, and elevate care work.
  • Democratize wealth and power: Tax wealth, expand public ownership, and support worker cooperatives.
  • Promote sufficiency: Support local food, durable goods, zero-waste design, and right-to-repair policies.
  • Embed ecological ethics: Embrace interdependence, regeneration, and reciprocity in all systems.

Why It Matters

This is not austerity—it’s redefined abundance: meeting everyone’s needs within planetary boundaries while honoring interdependence and ensuring a thriving world for future generations.
