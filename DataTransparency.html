Data, Monitoring, and Participatory Governance

Building Transparency and Community Power

Effective climate action requires not just good intentions, but robust data, monitoring, and governance. This plan centers transparency and community leadership to build trust, strengthen civic engagement, and ensure accountability.

Core Strategies

  • Open geospatial data platforms: Provide public access to land, environment, housing, and transit data.
  • Public dashboards: Track emissions, restoration, transit, housing, and environmental outcomes.
  • Participatory GIS and community mapping: Equip residents to document local priorities and challenges.
  • Participatory planning and budgeting: Give communities real power in decisions and funding allocations.
  • Accountability frameworks: Establish oversight boards, transparency mandates, and formal feedback mechanisms.

Why It Matters

Transparent, community-driven governance improves outcomes, builds public trust, and makes climate solutions more just and effective. Without formal mechanisms, open data alone is insufficient to shift power or deliver equitable results.

Integrated Strategy

These systems connect to all pillars of the climate plan—ensuring fair distribution of resources, tracking emissions and restoration progress, and elevating local voices in shaping policy and investments.
