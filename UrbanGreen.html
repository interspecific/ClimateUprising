Urban Greenspace and Urban Forestry

A Blueprint for Healthy, Resilient, and Equitable Cities

Urban greenspace and forestry are critical infrastructure, offering solutions for climate adaptation, health, environmental justice, and community resilience. This plan centers investments in historically marginalized neighborhoods to ensure that every resident has access to nature’s benefits.

Core Strategies

  • Plant one billion urban trees: Prioritize underserved, heat-vulnerable areas.
  • Guarantee greenspace access: Ensure every resident lives within ¼ mile of a park.
  • Convert vacant land: Transform lots and malls into parks, gardens, and meadows.
  • Establish ecological corridors: Connect habitats to support biodiversity and resilience.
  • Green every schoolyard: Create outdoor learning spaces with native plants.
  • Use native, diverse species: Prioritize ecological resilience over monocultures.
  • Enact tree preservation laws: Protect mature trees with local ordinances.
  • Monitor with geospatial tools: Track canopy cover, access, and heat vulnerability.

Why This Matters

Urban trees sequester carbon, lower temperatures, intercept stormwater, and improve air quality. But historically redlined communities have 23% less canopy, exacerbating health and climate disparities. Equitable urban greening is a public health, environmental, and racial justice imperative.

Integrated Strategy

Urban forestry complements land use reform, green transportation, environmental remediation, and green jobs programs—building healthier, cooler, and more equitable cities.
