Public Transportation Transformation

Building a Sustainable, Equitable Mobility System

The U.S. transportation system has long prioritized cars over people. This plan outlines a bold shift to a low-carbon, people-first system centered around equity, access, and environmental sustainability.

Core Strategies

  • Expand urban transit: High-frequency BRT, light rail, and streetcars in dense corridors.
  • Retrofit streets: Build complete streets for pedestrians, cyclists, and buses.
  • Electrify transit fleets: All buses and trains zero-emission by 2035.
  • Eliminate/reduce fares: Treat transit as a public good to boost ridership and equity.
  • Integrate first/last-mile connections: Connect transit with bikes, scooters, and shuttles.
  • National high-speed rail: Federal investment in fast, frequent rail across metro regions.
  • Modernize Amtrak corridors: Electrification, reliability upgrades, and more frequent service.
  • Create intermodal hubs: Seamless transfers between rail, bus, bike, and walking routes.
  • Ban short-haul flights: Where rail under 2.5 hours exists, prioritize rail over air travel.
  • Invest in underserved areas: Prioritize communities harmed by past transportation policy.

Why This Matters

Transportation is the U.S.'s largest source of GHG emissions. Public transit decarbonizes mobility, reduces air pollution, and makes cities healthier and more affordable. Car-free access increases equity and economic opportunity for all.

Integrated Strategy

This transformation connects with broader goals—land use reform, job creation, remediation, and public health—to build sustainable and just communities.
